# Partner reading list

A curated list of topics for the partner to read up on through the
pregnancy, organised by when each topic becomes most useful. Most
items are short (10–30 minute reads); a few are longer.

This file is the **canonical definition** of what "the partner reading
list" means in any weekly file's *For your partner* section. When a
weekly file says "keep momentum on the reading list" or "start the
reading list", that refers to this list. Don't reproduce the whole
list inline in a weekly file; instead, name 1–3 specific items
relevant to the current week.

## Why a reading list

The asymmetry of pregnancy attention is real: the pregnant person
absorbs an enormous amount about their own body and the developing
baby, often because they have to. The partner generally has time to
read — and the most useful partners by the third trimester are the
ones who can have informed conversations about birth choices without
their partner having to also be the expert.

The list leans on Evidence Based Birth heavily because EBB does the
unusual thing of presenting evidence with trade-offs rather than
prescribing. Most pieces don't take strong positions; they show what
the data says. NHS Best Start, RCOG patient leaflets, and AIMS are
the next-tier go-tos.

## The list

### First reads — birth fundamentals (introduce ~weeks 14–18)

- **The intervention cascade** — how one intervention in labour
  often leads to the next, and what opting out at each step looks
  like.
- **Continuous vs intermittent fetal monitoring** — the trade-offs.
  Cochrane evidence summarised by EBB.
- **Eating and drinking in labour** — the old "nothing by mouth"
  rule vs current evidence.
- **Pain relief options** — epidural, gas/air (Entonox), TENS, water,
  movement. Pros and cons of each, the timing of when each is
  available.

### Birth-day choices (~weeks 24–32)

- **Cord clamping** — delayed (60+ seconds, ideally longer) vs
  immediate, and the iron-stores evidence for delaying.
- **Vitamin K** — IM injection, oral, or decline. NHS, AAP, and EBB
  all useful.
- **Eye ointment** — routine where you are, what it's for, the
  trade-offs.
- **Pushing positions** — upright vs supine, evidence on perineal
  outcomes and labour duration.
- **Episiotomy** — when it's done routinely vs selectively, and
  the evidence on routine use.
- **Induction timing** — the ARRIVE study and the trade-offs of
  induction at 39 vs 41+ weeks.

### The golden hour and first 48 hours (~weeks 32–38)

- **The golden hour** — uninterrupted skin-to-skin in the first
  hour, why it matters for breastfeeding initiation,
  thermoregulation, and microbiome seeding.
- **Breastfeeding initiation** — what the first feed looks like,
  common difficulties, when to call for lactation help.
- **Newborn screening** — heel prick (Guthrie / NBS), hearing test,
  what's being checked.
- **Hep B at birth** — schedule recommendations and alternatives.
- **Perineal massage** — the actual technique, the evidence (Tier 1
  per the interventions review), how the partner can help from
  ~34 weeks.

### Mental health awareness (read anytime, surface in late pregnancy)

The single best partner-facing read on this is **`mental-health/perinatal-emotional-journey-eli5.md`** in the skill's own references — a plain-language summary of the full evidence brief (`perinatal-emotional-journey.md`) covering the normal/clinical/emergency layers, the intrusive-thoughts-vs-psychosis distinction, and what helps. Start there.

Specific topics to read up on:

- **Antenatal and postnatal depression** — symptoms, the Edinburgh Postnatal Depression Scale, treatment options. Both partners. Pooled prevalence ~12–18% (often "1 in 6") — common, treatable, undertreated.
- **Partner perinatal mental health** — pooled paternal depression ~8–10%, peaks 3–6 months postpartum. Often unrecognised. Postpartum Support International has a dedicated Help for Dads page (postpartum.net/get-help/help-for-dads) and the DadCare program.
- **The intrusive-thoughts-vs-psychosis distinction** — distressing intrusive thoughts of harm to the baby are normal in the majority of new mothers and not predictive of harm. The emergency is when the thought is *believed* and *not distressing*, often with severe insomnia and confusion in the first 2 weeks postpartum.
- **Postpartum psychosis** — rare (1–2 per 1,000) but a true emergency. Action on Postpartum Psychosis (app-network.org) is the best UK resource.
- **Matrescence** — Alexandra Sacks' framing of becoming a mother as an identity transition, useful for understanding mood and ambivalence. Pairs with the Hoekzema 2017 brain-remodeling research for the biological side.

### Logistics and recovery (~weeks 34+)

- **What postpartum recovery actually looks like** — bleeding
  (lochia), pain, fatigue, the "fourth trimester".
- **The mental load of early parenthood** — the asymmetry that
  often emerges in the first weeks if not pre-planned.
- **Sleep deprivation and shift planning** — what realistic
  shift-splits look like in the first six weeks.
- **Visitor and "village" management** — who to invite, when, and
  the politics of the first two weeks.

## Anchor sources

- **Evidence Based Birth** (evidencebasedbirth.com) — long-form
  articles and a podcast on most birth-decision topics.
- **NHS Best Start in Life** — for a calmer, less-prescriptive UK
  clinical view.
- **RCOG patient information leaflets** — UK clinical authority,
  good on specific procedures.
- **AIMS** (aims.org.uk) — informed-choice oriented, a useful
  counterweight to a paternalistic clinical framing.
- **Maternal Mental Health Alliance** — perinatal mental health
  for the UK context.
- **Postpartum Support International** — for postpartum mental
  health resources.
- **Alexandra Sacks** (TED talk + writing) — matrescence framing.

Don't reproduce links in the weekly files. Name the topic and
optionally an anchor source ("EBB has a clear primer on the
intervention cascade") and let the partner find their preferred
source.

## How to surface in weekly files

- **Weeks 14–18 (introduction window):** "start the partner reading
  list" — name 2–3 first-read items (intervention cascade, cord
  clamping, golden hour are good first picks).
- **Weeks 18–28 (deepening):** pick up specific items as they become
  relevant to that week's content. If discussing the birth plan,
  pull from birth-day choices. If discussing classes, pull from
  birth fundamentals.
- **Weeks 28–36 (birth focus):** birth-day choices and golden hour
  reads, paced with the birth-plan conversations.
- **Weeks 36+ (postpartum prep):** logistics and recovery,
  postpartum mental health, visitor management.
- **Any week (mental health awareness):** surface in the *What to
  say to her* detailed breakdown's partner-mental-health bit, or
  in *For your partner* when relevant.

The point is not for the partner to read everything. It's for them
to be informed enough by birth that they can carry their share of
the conversations, and informed enough by the postpartum window that
they can spot the warning signs in both their partner and themselves.
