# Pregnancy Diet: What to Actually Do, Trimester by Trimester

*A plain-English action guide based on ACOG, WHO, NHS, and NIH evidence.*

---

## The One-Sentence Version

Eat normal healthy food, take your prenatal vitamin, avoid a short list of risky foods, and don't stress about "eating for two" — your body barely needs extra food until halfway through.

---

## The Big Picture

Think of it like this: your body is building a tiny human from scratch. It needs specific raw materials (folate for the spine, iron for blood, calcium for bones, DHA for the brain). Your job is just to keep those materials coming in steadily — mostly through food, with a supplement as insurance.

You're **not** eating for two. You're eating for 1.0 in the first trimester, 1.15 in the second, and 1.2 in the third. That's it.

---

## Before Pregnancy / As Soon as You Know

**Do these immediately:**
- ✅ Start a **prenatal vitamin** (the folic acid matters most right now — baby's spine forms by day 28, often before you even know you're pregnant)
- ✅ Stop **alcohol** completely — there is no safe amount
- ✅ Cut caffeine to **≤1 cup of coffee/day** (≤200 mg)
- ✅ Stop eating **raw fish, rare meat, soft cheeses like brie/camembert**

## First Trimester (Weeks 1–13)

### What's happening
Baby's spine, brain, and major organs are forming. This is the highest-stakes trimester for nutrient quality (not quantity).

### Calories
**No extra food needed.** Eat the same amount as before — about 1,800 kcal/day.

### Action Steps

| Do This | Why | How |
|---------|-----|-----|
| Take prenatal vitamin daily | Folate prevents spinal defects; iron builds blood | Set a phone alarm; take with food if nauseated |
| Eat eggs (2/day is fine) | Best source of choline (brain building) | Scrambled, hard-boiled, in omelette — must be fully cooked |
| Eat dark leafy greens | Natural folate + iron + calcium | Spinach in smoothie, salad, or sautéed |
| Eat some fish (low-mercury) | DHA for baby's brain | Salmon, sardines, or trout 2×/week |
| Stay hydrated | Blood volume starting to rise | 8–10 glasses water/day |

### If Nausea Is Killing You
- Eat small amounts often (empty stomach = worse nausea)
- Plain crackers, toast, or ginger tea before getting out of bed
- Vitamin B6 (10–25 mg, 3×/day) can help — ask your doctor
- Avoid strong smells, greasy/spicy food
- It's OK if you can only eat bland carbs for a few weeks — baby is tiny and draws from your stores

### Avoid
- 🚫 Alcohol (zero)
- 🚫 Soft/mould-ripened cheese (brie, camembert, blue cheese) — listeria risk
- 🚫 Raw or pink meat — toxoplasmosis
- 🚫 Liver or liver pâté — too much vitamin A, can cause birth defects
- 🚫 Swordfish, shark, marlin — mercury damages baby's brain
- 🚫 Raw shellfish
- 🚫 Cold smoked salmon / gravlax (unless cooked hot)

## Second Trimester (Weeks 14–27)

### What's happening
Baby is growing fast — bones hardening, brain expanding, body filling out. Nausea usually lifts. You get hungry.

### Calories
**Add ~340 extra kcal/day.** That's roughly:
- A banana + handful of almonds + yogurt, OR
- A peanut butter sandwich, OR
- A bowl of oatmeal with fruit

Not a whole extra meal. Just one solid snack.

### Action Steps

| Do This | Why | How |
|---------|-----|-----|
| Keep taking prenatal | Iron needs ramp up as blood volume surges | Same routine as before |
| Boost calcium to 1,000 mg/day | Baby's skeleton is mineralizing now | 3 servings dairy/day (milk, yogurt, cheese) OR fortified plant milk + tofu |
| Eat iron-rich foods | Blood volume expanding ~50% | Red meat 2–3×/week, or beans/lentils + spinach (pair with vitamin C for absorption) |
| Get vitamin D | Helps absorb all that calcium | Fortified milk, fatty fish, some sunlight |
| Continue DHA fish 2×/week | Brain growth accelerating | Salmon, sardines, herring |
| Add whole grains + fiber | Prevents constipation (very common now) | Oats, brown rice, whole wheat bread, beans |

### Practical Tips
- If you don't eat dairy: fortified plant milk + calcium-set tofu + broccoli + almonds can cover calcium. But check amounts — you need 1,000 mg total.
- If you don't eat fish: take a separate DHA/algae supplement (200–300 mg/day).
- Iron from plants is harder to absorb. Squeeze lemon on spinach/beans (vitamin C helps). Don't take iron with coffee/tea/calcium — they block absorption.

### Still Avoid
Same list as first trimester — the risks don't go away. Plus:
- 🚫 Caffeine still ≤200 mg/day (1 filter coffee or 2 teas)
- 🚫 Still no alcohol

## Third Trimester (Weeks 28–40)

### What's happening
Baby is packing on weight, brain is wiring rapidly, lungs are maturing. You feel huge and probably have heartburn.

### Calories
**Add ~450 extra kcal/day** (total). That's about one extra small meal or two healthy snacks beyond your pre-pregnancy baseline.

### Action Steps

| Do This | Why | How |
|---------|-----|-----|
| Keep taking prenatal | Iron is critical — anemia peaks now (affects 27% of women in 3rd trimester) | If constipated, try taking at bedtime or split dose |
| Continue high-calcium foods | Skeleton finishing mineralization | Same 3 dairy servings or equivalent |
| Eat protein at every meal | Baby gaining ~½ pound/week | Eggs, fish, chicken, beans, tofu, Greek yogurt |
| Eat smaller, more frequent meals | Stomach is compressed; heartburn is real | 5–6 mini-meals instead of 3 large ones |
| Keep fiber + water high | Constipation and hemorrhoids are common | Prunes, oats, water, movement |
| Continue DHA | Brain wiring happening fast | Fish or algae supplement |

### Heartburn Strategies
- Avoid eating 2–3 hours before bed
- Small portions
- Avoid spicy/acidic/fried food if it triggers you
- Prop yourself up slightly when sleeping

### Still Avoid
Same list — hold the line through delivery.

## Quick-Reference: The "Always" List

### Always Do (All Trimesters)
- ✅ Prenatal vitamin every day
- ✅ Folate-rich foods (greens, beans, fortified bread)
- ✅ Iron-rich foods (meat, beans, fortified cereal)
- ✅ Calcium-rich foods (dairy, fortified plant milk, tofu)
- ✅ Low-mercury fish 2×/week (salmon, sardines, trout)
- ✅ Eggs for choline
- ✅ Plenty of water (8–12 cups/day)
- ✅ Fruits and vegetables (variety = variety of nutrients)
- ✅ Iodized salt when cooking

### Always Avoid
- 🚫 Alcohol — zero, the whole time
- 🚫 Shark, swordfish, marlin (mercury)
- 🚫 Raw/rare meat (toxoplasmosis)
- 🚫 Soft mould-ripened and blue cheeses unless cooked piping hot
- 🚫 Liver (excess vitamin A → birth defects)
- 🚫 Raw shellfish
- 🚫 Unpasteurized milk/juice
- 🚫 Caffeine >200 mg/day

## If You're Vegetarian or Vegan

You can absolutely have a healthy pregnancy — but you need to be more deliberate:

| Nutrient | Problem | Fix |
|----------|---------|-----|
| B12 | Zero in plant foods; deficiency → nerve damage | Supplement (at least 2.6 mcg/day, many doctors suggest higher) |
| Iron | Plant iron absorbs poorly | Beans + lemon/pepper (vitamin C); maybe extra iron supplement |
| DHA | No fish = no DHA | Algae-based DHA supplement (200–300 mg/day) |
| Calcium | If no dairy | Fortified soy milk, calcium-set tofu, kale, broccoli |
| Iodine | If no dairy or fish | Iodized salt or supplement |
| Zinc | Lower absorption from plants | Legumes, seeds, whole grains; soak/sprout to improve absorption |

## What About Prenatal Vitamins Specifically?

Not all prenatals are created equal. Here's what to check on the label:

| Must Have | Amount | Watch Out |
|-----------|--------|-----------|
| Folic acid | 400–800 mcg | ✓ Most have this |
| Iron | 27+ mg | ⚠️ Gummies often skip this |
| Vitamin D | 600–1,000 IU | ✓ Most have this |
| Iodine | 150–220 mcg | ⚠️ Many brands skip this |
| DHA | 200+ mg | ⚠️ Usually needs separate supplement |
| Choline | 100–450 mg | ⚠️ Most brands have little or none |

**Tip:** Look for USP or NSF third-party tested labels. A 2023 government report found that 11 of 12 prenatals tested had nutrients that didn't match the label amount.

## The "Don't Worry" Section

- You don't need to eat perfectly. A varied diet + prenatal vitamin covers you.
- If all you could eat in the first trimester was crackers and ginger ale — that's OK. Baby is the size of a pea and took what it needed from your reserves.
- An occasional cup of coffee (under 200 mg) is fine. The real risk starts above 350 mg/day.
- Hard cheeses (cheddar, parmesan, gruyere) are safe — it's only the soft mould-ripened ones to avoid.
- Sushi with cooked fish or vegetable rolls is fine. Just skip the raw fish.
- You don't need to buy expensive "pregnancy superfoods." Regular salmon, eggs, spinach, beans, and yogurt are superfoods enough.
